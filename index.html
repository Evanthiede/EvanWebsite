Intro

Hi, my name is Evan and I'm a game developer. I have spent the last 4 years in this program developing my skill in a wide variety of mediums. These range from video editing and audio design, all the way up to Amazon Alexa integration with Echo Frames. While I'd like to say that I've mastered all of those, I gravitated more towards the visual elements.

I focused heavily on the game developemtn side of my program. We were introduced to Unity pretty early on and I took a liking to it. I chose to focus on the level design and flow aspects of game development. I spent my time building out greybox scenes and eventually filling them in with finalized assets. I lumped UI/UX elements that we were focusing on into this focus. Instead of making code driven complex mechanics, I stuck to environmental storytelling and provoking emotions. To see them for yourself, here they are..

Work

Echoes of Gold

Echoes of Gold is the culmination of my development as a level designer. You explore an unknown environment that you are dropped into suddenly. Forced to wander, you are greeted with rolling golden grass covered islands in a canyon filled with fog. Runes in a foreign language are littered across the walls and decorate interactive pillars.


Echoes of Gold was a project that I really wanted to make the player feel at peace, but also mildy uneasy. The lack of understanding and the sheer solitary nature of environment creates the feeling that you are somewhere that you are not supposed to be.

World Building

There were a few environments that were created with the intention of being a part of Echoes, but they just didn't fit. These environments are now just artifacts of potential world building.


Finger Gun

Finger Gun was my first real step into Unreal Engine 5. This was a game that I created with a team to take a funny spin on a showdown style shootoff. The players would shoot explosive spheres out of a finger gun, the rest is fairly standard.

Alongside these projects, I am part of a small studio called Torchfire. My primary role is the level design lead, at this stage I'm basically building out levels and mapping out puzzle flows for the time when we get to implimentation.


Animations

Of all the skills that I have, Blender is the one that I am most interested in learning more about. I've fiddled around with a lot of files and done some basic renders, but I know that I'm only scratching the surface of what I could be making.
